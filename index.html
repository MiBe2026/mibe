How we got here.
Spoiler: not in a straight line.

BEng (Hons) International Computing Science. Graduated with a 2:2, which is the respectable way of saying "I had other priorities sometimes." A year off first. No regrets.

🎓 2:2 and proud

Early career

Graduate scheme. Then contracting.

Started at Capita on a graduate scheme doing software testing. Did that for two years, finished it, then went contracting. Financial institutions, mostly. The work was fine. The commute was fine. Everything was fine.

🏦 fine

2020

Everything stopped.

Lockdown. And then something broke. Not the kind of thing you walk off — a proper mental breakdown. The kind where you have to make a choice about what comes next, and "more of the same" isn't one of the options.

If you're going through something similar: reach out. Seriously. Mind and Samaritans exist for exactly this.

🧠 it gets better

Post-breakdown

CSCS card. Boots. A shovel.

Instead of going back to a desk, got a CSCS card and went to work on construction sites as a labourer. Started from the bottom — general labourer, then power tools, then skilled labourer. Turns out physical work and a change of scene does something good for the brain.

🏗️ actual fresh air

Now

Material controller. And then some.

Moved into materials control, where the IT background started pulling its weight again. Looked at how sites manage materials. Decided it was a mess. Started building IPOS — initially as a hobby, currently as something that might actually matter.

🟠 this is the one

// ipos

It's Probably On Site.

Materials management on construction sites is a mess.
Spreadsheets. WhatsApp. Guesswork.
IPOS is what happens when a software engineer gets a CSCS card
and decides to actually fix the problem.

📍

Know where it is

Track materials on site in real time. Not "probably by the hoarding." Actually on site.

📦

Know what you have

Deliveries logged. Stock tracked. No more walking the site to count bricks.

🔧

Built by someone on site

Not a product manager's idea of what site workers need. Built by one, for them.

Currently in development — initially targeting Vistry Group. Licensing available.
